The Hunter

Description Personal Contributions Technical Details Challenges Outcome

Description

An Acerola Game Jam 0 submission. Given the theme of aberration, I decided to make a game about literal aberrations: the trolls (enemies in the game), with the player's mission to destroy them. I also used this game as an academic project for my Work-Based Learning module, demonstrating my ability to work under strict time constraints and achieve a good overall outcome.

Personal Contributions

I designed and implemented mechanics and systems, such as AI, combat, movement, spawning, audio, saving and loading, and even a tiny settings menu.

Technical Details

With only two weeks for the game jam, I knew I had time to create something ambitious, but I still needed to plan how I would manage my time during development. I decided to concentrate on the game's design and programming while outsourcing the art. Most notably, I used the publicly available Kingdom assets from Noio, which were utilised in the original Kingdom flash game.

Challenges

When I created this game, I was not an advanced programmer; it took me some time to develop the water reflection shader and the enemy AI. However, continuous playtesting and debugging helped me resolve issues such as the AI not attacking or ignoring the player, among other problems.

Outcome

The game received an overall positive review from players, and although I received some fair criticism for certain game design choices, I am quite happy with what I produced, given the time I had and the other projects I was working on.
